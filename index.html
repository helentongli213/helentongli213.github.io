PUBLICITY OFFICER
Berkeley ANova, Berkeley CA, September 2017 - Present
ANova is a student organization dedicated to improving computer science education in under-resourced communities across the Bay Area. Members go on weekly visits to middle and high schools to mentor young students in computer science. Every semester, ANova also hosts ANova Hacks, a 10-hour hackathon for high school students to come together and create interesting and unique projects.
As the publicity officer of Berkeley ANova, my primary responsibility includes leading a committee of six club members in providing design, social media, and historian services to the organization. I also opened up opportunities for my committee members to grow professionally through personally-led design and marketing workshops, resume reviews, and portfolio assistance. My responsibilities outside my committee members include designing for larger events like recruitment and ANova Hacks as well as collaborating with other officers in terms of ANova's presence both on and off campus.
SOCIAL SLICE MEMBER
UC Berkeley Social Media Team, Berkeley CA, September 2017 - Present
Social Slice is UC Berkeley's student-led social media advisory team, responsible for all of the university's social media platforms including Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, and Snapchat.
As a member of Social Slice, my weekly responsibilities included storyboarding and posting Snapchat stories as well as proposing Facebook posts for the official UC Berkeley Facebook page. These both reached an audience of over 5,000 for every post. Additionally, I interviewed various UC Berkeley staff for the #BerkeleyMinds campaign, assisted in writing and publicizing for our #CALoween event, and worked with other Social Slice members to implement student-specific events like Cal Day.
WEB DEVELOPER
The Daily Californian, Berkeley CA, September 2017 - December 2017
The Daily Californian is an independent, student-run newspaper that serves the University of California, Berkeley campus and its surrounding community. The Daily Californian's web development team was specifically formed in the fall of 2017 in an effort to modernize UC Berkeley's leading student newspaper.
In the web development team, I worked closely with a group of five other students to completely, from scratch, rewrite The Daily Californian's website. As a team, we were expected to provide a student's perspective on both the website's interface aesthetic and its usability. Several of my personal assignments included re-aligning the website's header, designing a new front page, and providing new documentation to assist any future endeavors to edit the code.
