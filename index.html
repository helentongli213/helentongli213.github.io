ASSISTANT INSTRUCTOR
Galileo Learning, San Jose CA, June - August 2017
Designed for burgeoning visionaries, Galileo Summer Quest empowers 5th – 8th graders to take the next step. Campers choose from 13+ immersive majors, picking something they already love or a new area they're excited to explore, then spend the week diving in.
As an assistant instructor for the Video Game Design and MOD Design with Minecraft majors, I was responsible for knowing my lead instructor's needs ahead of time, maintaining classroom conduct in a weekly-changing group of 5th-8th graders, and fostering a passion for computer design in youth. I also took on additional responsibilities like substituting for my lead instructor when he was absent, introducing other outlets of CS exploration unmentioned in the curriculum, and providing mentorship to younger students to encourage them not only in their CS education, but in their development as innovators and creators. Working at Galileo Summer Quest allowed me to combine my passions of computer science and youth education while providing me with opportunities to grow professionally.

MAIN STREET POKÉ: MENU DESIGN
Main Street Poké is a family-owned restaurant in Carmel, Indiana. I worked closely with the business owner to create a set of coherent menu designs to assist customers who had never tried poké before. These designs currently decorate the walls and menu screens of the small shop in Carmel.
DESINV 198: INTRODUCTION TO PHOTOSHOP AND ILLUSTRATOR
This introductory course teaches graphic design through the use of Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator. Lessons are built for students who do not have any prior experience or knowledge of these programs. A a final project, students were asked to create a detailed infographic using Illustrator or Photoshop techniques.
COMPSCI 198: WEB DESIGN DECAL
Web Design DeCal (WDD) is a 2-unit course at UC Berkeley that teaches students how to build a beautiful website. The material is limited to front-end web development. As a final project, students were asked to create a website functional on the front-end scale using HTML, CSS, and JQuery techniques.
COMPSCI 10: THE BEAUTY AND JOY OF COMPUTING
The Beauty and Joy of Computing provides an introduction to the history, social implications, great principles, and future of computing. The final project involved using Snap! (a purely graphical programming language) to produce something interesting, useful, and challenging (in this case, an educational video game).
