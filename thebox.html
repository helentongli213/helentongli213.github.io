TheBox

Prompt:How can we bring more awareness to the general population of how we portray ourselves on image-based social media (ie. Instagram)?

Solution:People tend to justify their use of image-based social media with the phrase, "An image is worth a thousand words." In other words, for example, if I have 50 images on my social media account, then these images must thus tell a 50,000 word story about me as an individual. TheBox aims to question this mindset by condensing the collection of one person's Instagram images into five tangible objects, forcing us to reconsider the genuine depth of our social media personas.

Method:Our group began by conducting culture probes ranging from captioning random images, editing photos for Instagram, and journaling their thoughts on social media. From this, we ideated the process of TheBox and created a sample product. Using my personal Instagram account, we ran the images through an API that did a reverse Google image search and returned a list of associating nouns. We then surveyed a group of my social media "friends" and asked them to choose 5 nouns they thought best exemplified me. For each of these 5 nouns, we found a representative object which we then placed in a nondescript box that, theoretically, would be sent to the user with no context.
